Fire

Fire is known to be destructive and deadly but with its colors, we see it as warmth and comforting. Orange is a secondary color. secondary colors are mixed with primary colors. Red and yellow make orange and within that are more desciptive emotions that we can connect with.

Water

Water is known to be flowly and calm. But there can be other factors that effects the motion. Storms can cause tsunamis and flooding. But water is also an important source for humans. We drink it to survive. Colors associated are blues and whites. Blues are a typical color that I like to mention as calm and flowy. White can symbolise new beginnings. As usual. Blue and white can make lighter shades as lighter emotions.

Air

Air is like water but breath-y. Literally. We need air to breathe. Air is special because wind is a weather example of the element. It can be calm like on a beach day with sun. Or it can be violent and cause loose objects and buildings to be swept into a tornado and destroy. I like to use pastels for wind. The color palettes are calming and peaceful. They also provide a visual gradient to the eye as it can match our breathing patterns.

Earth

Earth. It's a planet. It is home to us. It is magical. Earth's creations are truly unique and have created some of the most beautiful landscapes. Mountains, rainforests, and ecosystems for animals and humans. Earth on a color palette is greens and browns resembling the ground and life. Greens are like blues. They're calming. But also eye catching. Greens are a secondary color. Blues and yellows make greens. Emotionally, green can be peaceful like blues or deceiving and manipulative. Think of any Disney villan. There is green around them. Green can be evil. Browns are seen as dull but are used daily as a default color for a lot of things like home projects or clothing. Earth can be balanced and safe where we can explore. Yet, Earth can also be deadly. We have earthquakes which cause extreme damage. Whether or not, Earth also can be kind or evil. You'd never know what happens.
